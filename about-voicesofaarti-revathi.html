Revathi

Finding her voice through every page she read

← Back to all stories

Revathi arrived at Aarti Home unable to read a single word. She was ten years old and had never set foot in a school. Within her first year, she was racing through every book she could find — and writing her own short stories in a notebook kept under her mattress.

Her teachers encouraged her to share her writing, and soon she was reading stories aloud at Aarti's cultural programmes. She was shy at first, her voice barely above a whisper. But the applause gave her something she had never had: confidence.

Revathi completed her secondary schooling and enrolled in a journalism diploma. She dreams of becoming a reporter who tells the stories of girls and communities the mainstream media overlooks.

“I used to think I had no voice. Then I learned to read, and I realised I had a thousand things to say.”

Revathi's journey from illiteracy to aspiring journalist is a reminder of what becomes possible when a child is given books, encouragement, and a safe place to dream.

Revathi is completing her journalism diploma and has already had two articles published in a regional newspaper.
