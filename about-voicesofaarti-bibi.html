Bibi (Head Cook)

Bibi’s Kitchen – from firewood to feeding futures

← Back to all stories

My husband would never provide for our family of five. All the money he made would go straight into his alcohol addiction, so I had to earn instead. I would climb a steep hill every day to collect firewood and sell it at a market throughout the day.

At this time, I discovered my passion for cooking; I would make snacks to sell at the city market along with the firewood and by the end of the day, they would have been sold out!

My husband’s word was the law — one day, he decided that he wanted to stop our girls’ education and have them help out at home to pay for their brothers’ school books. It was heartbreaking because none of us could fight him on it.

He passed away from alcohol poisoning soon after, leaving us to fend for ourselves. My daughters had little education, and I could already see my sons starting to get distracted with their jobs.

When Sandhyamma heard of our predicament (and tasted my snacks), she offered for me to become Aarti Home’s head cook! And here I am!

My favourite part of being the cook is that I get to indulge the young girls in whatever kinds of food they love! I can’t imagine not working this job! My girls have also found jobs as tailors and are now making a lot of money. We are all so grateful to Sandhyamma for all her effort and love.
