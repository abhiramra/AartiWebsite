Voices of Aarti

Keerthana

Her songs carry stories the world needs to hear

← Back to all stories

Keerthana has a voice that stops conversations in rooms. She has sung for as long as she can remember — humming to herself as a child even when life gave her little to sing about. When she arrived at Aarti, her voice was the first thing she shared with the community, immediately bringing people together.

Aarti supported her music training and helped her perform at regional cultural events. She won two state-level competitions for young folk singers and used the recognition to apply for a formal music scholarship.

Keerthana now studies classical music and performs at awareness events about women's empowerment — using her talent as a platform for the causes she cares about most.

“Music was the only thing entirely mine when I had nothing else. Now it lets me speak for girls who have not yet found their voice.”

Keerthana's music is both a personal achievement and a form of advocacy — a reminder that when art is nurtured, it becomes a force for change.

Keerthana has recorded a short album of original songs telling the stories of girls and women from her community, and hopes to release it more widely soon.
