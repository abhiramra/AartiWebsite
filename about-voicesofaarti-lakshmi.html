Lakshmi

From hardship to healer

← Back to all stories

Lakshmi grew up in difficult circumstances, the daughter of a daily-wage labourer who struggled to provide even the basics. When she was brought to Aarti as a child, she was malnourished and had never attended school. Within a year, she was reading voraciously.

What set Lakshmi apart was her compassion. She would spend her free time sitting with younger children who were unwell, talking softly and fetching water for them. The staff noticed this instinct early and encouraged her to pursue nursing.

After completing her schooling with support from Aarti, Lakshmi trained as a nurse and now works at a district hospital in Andhra Pradesh.

“When I was sick as a child, no one came to help me. I decided I would spend my life being that person for others.”

Lakshmi's career in healthcare is a direct expression of the empathy she developed at Aarti Home — a compassion born from her own experience of vulnerability.

Lakshmi visits Aarti regularly, conducting health check-ups for the children and inspiring younger girls who dream of careers in medicine.
