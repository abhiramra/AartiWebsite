Hemalatha

The lotus that rose from difficult waters

← Back to all stories

Hemalatha means 'lotus creeper' — a plant known for flowering in difficult conditions. It is a name that fits her perfectly. When she arrived at Aarti, she was quiet and withdrawn, carrying years of instability. Slowly, steadily, she began to bloom.

She found her joy in dance — a traditional form she learned at Aarti's cultural programme. What began as a shy shuffle at the back of the group became a confident, expressive practice she performed at regional cultural festivals.

Dance gave Hemalatha not just a skill but a way of expressing things that words could not. She trained as a dance teacher and now teaches classical dance to children in her community.

“In dance I found the courage I didn't know I had. My body learned to be brave before my voice did.”

Hemalatha's journey through dance is a reminder that healing and growth take many forms — and that the arts can reach places that other interventions cannot.

Hemalatha continues to perform and teach, and recently established a small scholarship fund for girls in her studio who cannot afford fees.
