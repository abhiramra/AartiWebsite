The Mana Bidda Project

Reaching every child in the community

← Back to all stories

Mana Bidda — 'our children' in Telugu — is Aarti's community outreach programme, reaching girls and boys who are at risk but not yet in residential care. Through trained outreach workers, Aarti connects with families in remote villages, providing education support, nutrition, health screenings, and child protection awareness.

The programme began after Aarti realised that for every child who reached its doors, hundreds more remained in the community without support. Workers now visit over two dozen villages regularly, identifying children who are out of school or in vulnerable situations.

Mana Bidda also runs bridge-learning camps for children who have fallen behind, and provides families with guidance on child rights and keeping girls in school.

“We cannot save every child by bringing them inside. We have to go out and change the world around them too.”

Since its launch, the Mana Bidda Project has engaged with thousands of children and their families — preventing many from entering crisis in the first place.

Aarti For Girls continues to expand the programme, with the goal of making every village in Y.S.R. District a safer, more supportive place for girls to grow.
