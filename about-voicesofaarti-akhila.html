Akhila

Complete in every way she chooses to be

← Back to all stories

Akhila's name means 'complete' in Sanskrit. She used to find that ironic — as a child she felt incomplete in every way, without a stable home, without education, without anyone who consistently believed in her. Aarti changed that sense of incompleteness, piece by piece.

She discovered a passion for science at Aarti's school and was encouraged to pursue it seriously. She went on to study chemistry at college, becoming one of the first girls from her village to earn a science degree.

Today Akhila works as a lab technician at a medical diagnostics centre — meticulous, curious, and deeply proud of the career she has built.

“I used to think I was missing something that everyone else was born with. Then I understood — I had everything I needed. I just needed the chance to use it.”

Akhila's scientific career is a reminder that potential is not determined by circumstance — it is revealed when circumstances are changed.

Akhila is now saving towards a postgraduate qualification in biochemistry and remains in close contact with her mentors at Aarti.
