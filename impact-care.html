Care: The Heart of Aarti

At Aarti, Care is not just a program; it's the very foundation of what we do, whether within the vibrant community of Aarti Village or through the loving support of our family-based care network.

For girls who have nowhere else to turn – often facing unimaginable hardship like being orphaned or escaping domestic abuse – Aarti Village is a true home. Within our sustainable, green campus, 125 girls live as sisters in small cottages, each guided by a dedicated house mother who fosters a loving family unit.

Bhanu's Story
Brought to Aarti Home at the tender age of two by her uncle after the tragic murder of her mother, Bhanu's early world was marked by profound loss. However, within the nurturing embrace of the Aarti community, she discovered not just shelter but a loving family and boundless opportunities to flourish. With a growing passion for biology and a vibrant involvement in extracurricular activities, she now actively pursues her dream of becoming a doctor.
Family Based Care — Nurturing Dreams Within a Mother's Love

In 2018, our Family Based Care program was born. Here, we provide crucial support for education, nutrition, and basic necessities, enabling children to stay within the love and security of their own homes.

  1. Our volunteers and social workers identify at-risk children from slums.
  2. Our social workers conduct a thorough investigation and assess the home environment for safety.
  3. Parents are encouraged to involve themselves in the safety and future of their children by enrolling their child in formal education.
  4. The child is enrolled in the school and provided food, clothing, medical and psychological care and education, and transportation to and from the school.
  5. Monthly visits by our teachers to the child's home to check on the well being of the child and family.
