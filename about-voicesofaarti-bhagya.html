Bhagya

Rewriting the story her name was given

← Back to all stories

Bhagya means fate in Telugu. Growing up, it felt like her fate had already been written — early marriage, no school, endless domestic work. But Bhagya refused to accept that story. When she came to Aarti as a teenager, she made a promise to herself: she would choose a different life.

At Aarti she discovered a talent for drawing and craft. Staff encouraged her and enrolled her in vocational art training. Soon she was producing intricate work that caught the attention of a crafts NGO, which offered her an apprenticeship.

Bhagya now runs a small craft enterprise from her home, selling handmade goods at local markets and online, and employs two other women from her neighbourhood.

“People named me after fate. But I have learned that fate is something you can change with your own hands.”

Bhagya's story challenges the notion that destiny is fixed — and shows what becomes possible when a girl is given time, training, and belief in herself.

Bhagya is now a mentor in Aarti's Empower programme, passing on her skills to girls learning vocational crafts.
