Manjula

Stitching together a new beginning

← Back to all stories

Manjula was brought to Aarti at twelve after her mother's illness left the family unable to care for the children. She was the eldest of three siblings and had spent years trying to be their caretaker. At Aarti, for the first time in a long while, she was allowed to simply be a child.

She threw herself into tailoring classes at Aarti's vocational centre, quickly mastering blouse patterns and eventually creating her own designs. Her work was picked up by the stitching cooperative Aarti partners with.

After completing her schooling, Manjula set up a small tailoring unit in her hometown with a micro-loan arranged through Aarti, and now earns a steady income.

“Every piece of cloth I stitch is proof that something broken can be made beautiful again.”

Manjula's tailoring business is both a livelihood and a symbol — of patience, of craft, and of a life rebuilt thread by thread.

Manjula has since trained three other women in her community in basic tailoring, expanding the ripple effect of what she learned at Aarti.
