Manikyamma

A grandmother's resilience, a family's hope

← Back to all stories

Manikyamma came to Aarti not as a child but as a grandmother — the primary caregiver of two granddaughters after her own children passed away. With no income and no support network, she feared the girls would be taken from her. Aarti's family-based care programme stepped in to help the family stay together.

Through Aarti's support, the granddaughters were enrolled in school and Manikyamma received skills training and livelihood assistance. The family received nutritional support and regular check-ins from a social worker.

Over time Manikyamma grew into a figure of quiet authority in her community, and neighbours began approaching her for advice about their own children.

“As long as I have breath, these children will have a home. That is the promise I made — and Aarti helped me keep it.”

Manikyamma's story is central to Aarti's understanding that protecting a child sometimes means supporting the family around her — not separating them.

Both of Manikyamma's granddaughters are now in secondary school and performing well, and the family continues to receive periodic support from Aarti's outreach team.
