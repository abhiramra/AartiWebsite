Prasad

A young man shaped by Aarti's values

← Back to all stories

Prasad grew up near Aarti Home and watched its work from the outside — watching girls who once seemed lost discover themselves and go on to build remarkable lives. Through Aarti's community outreach programme he received educational support and mentoring during his secondary schooling.

He was struck by how the programme treated the girls with respect and ambition, expecting the best from them when the rest of the world expected the least. He decided he wanted to work in a field where he could do the same for others.

Prasad now works in child welfare administration, helping to coordinate government services for at-risk families.

“I grew up believing girls needed to be managed and married. Aarti taught me they needed to be trusted and invested in. It changed how I see everything.”

Prasad's journey shows how Aarti's work transforms not just those directly in its care — but the communities and people around them.

Prasad volunteers regularly at Aarti's community events and has become a vocal advocate for girls' education in local government forums.
